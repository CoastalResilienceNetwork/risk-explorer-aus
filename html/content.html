Percentage of the population within a census block that are likely dependent on others during a flood event. Based on 2011 Census. High is >50%, Medium is 25 -50%, and Low is 0 – 25%.

High concentrations of population within a flood risk area are places where floods could impact the most people if not mitigated. Based on 2020 gridded population of the world, where quantiles High is > 200, Medium is 40-200, and Low is <40

Lower income households may lack the resources to protect themselves from a flood event and may need recovery assistance. Based on 2011 Census where High is <= 30th percentile, Medium is 40-70th percentile, and Low is > 70th percentile.

Land with high susceptibility to erode from surface water movement. Derived from land quality associated with soil-landscape by the Dept of Agriculture. High is >= 50%, Medium is 10-50%, and Low is <=10%

Land with high susceptibility for excess surface water to pond based on average seasonal rainfall, from the Dept of Agriculture. High is >= 50%, Medium is 10-50%, and Low is <=10%

Land with high susceptibility to flood due to overflowing streams and/or precipitation run off, from the Dept of Agriculture. High is >= 50%, Medium is 10-50%, and Low is <=10%

Estuarine land near projected flood extents is at highest risk of future flooding as sea levels rise. High = Within 500m of flood extent, Medium is within 500-1,000m, and Low is within 1,000-1,500m.
